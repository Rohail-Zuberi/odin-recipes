Curry


This is a special south asian dish called Curry Pakora

Curry pakora is a type of Pakistani snack that is made by coating bite-sized pieces of chicken, fish, or vegetables in a spicy batter and deep-frying them until they are crispy and golden-brown. The batter for curry pakora typically includes a blend of flour, spices, and herbs, such as cumin, coriander, turmeric, chili powder, and cilantro. The finished dish is typically served with chutney or a dipping sauce, and it is often enjoyed as an appetizer or a snack. Curry pakora is known for its spicy, flavorful taste and is a popular street food in Pakistan.

Ingredients

Here is a general recipe for curry pakora that you can try:

  • 1 pound of chicken, fish, or vegetables (such as potatoes, onions, and bell peppers)
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of chickpea flour
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon of chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1/4 cup of chopped cilantro
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Cut the chicken, fish, or vegetables into bite-sized pieces.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, chickpea flour, cumin, coriander, turmeric, chili powder, salt, and baking powder. Mix well to make the batter.
  3. Stir in the chopped cilantro and water, mixing until the batter is smooth and has a thick, creamy consistency.
  4. Heat the vegetable oil in a deep-fryer or a large pot over medium-high heat.
  5. Dip the chicken, fish, or vegetables into the batter, making sure they are evenly coated. Carefully lower them into the hot oil and fry for 3-4 minutes, until they are crispy and golden-brown.
  6. Remove the curry pakora from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain them on paper towels to remove any excess oil.
  7. Serve the curry pakora hot, with chutney or dipping sauce on the side.
