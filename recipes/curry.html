This is a special south asian dish called Curry Pakora

Curry pakora is a type of Pakistani snack that is made by coating bite-sized pieces of chicken, fish, or vegetables in a spicy batter and deep-frying them until they are crispy and golden-brown. The batter for curry pakora typically includes a blend of flour, spices, and herbs, such as cumin, coriander, turmeric, chili powder, and cilantro. The finished dish is typically served with chutney or a dipping sauce, and it is often enjoyed as an appetizer or a snack. Curry pakora is known for its spicy, flavorful taste and is a popular street food in Pakistan.

Ingredients

There are many different variations of biryani, and the ingredients and method of preparation can vary depending on the region and personal preference. However, here is a general recipe for biryani that you can try:

  • 2 cups of long-grain basmati rice
  • 1 pound of chicken, lamb, or beef
  • 1 cup of yogurt
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of ginger paste
  • 1 tablespoon of biryani masala (a blend of spices)
  • 1 teaspoon of turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cardamom
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon of saffron (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of ghee or vegetable oil
  • 2 cups of water

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice well and soak it in water for at least 30 minutes.
  2. In a bowl, combine the yogurt, onion, garlic, ginger, biryani masala, turmeric, cumin, coriander, cardamom, cinnamon, and saffron (if using). Mix well to make a marinade.
  3. Coat the meat or vegetables with the marinade and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  4. In a large pot, heat the ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the marinated meat or vegetables and saut√© for 5-7 minutes, until they are lightly browned.
  5. Drain the rice and add it to the pot. Stir well to mix the rice with the meat or vegetables.
  6. Add the water to the pot and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with a lid, and let the biryani simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the liquid has been absorbed.
  7. Serve the biryani hot, with raita on the side.
