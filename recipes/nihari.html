Nihari


This is a special south asian dish called Nihari

Nihari is a type of Pakistani stew that is made with slow-cooked beef or lamb, and a mixture of spices. The dish is named after the Persian word "nihar," which means "morning," as it was traditionally eaten for breakfast. Nihari is typically made by simmering the meat for several hours until it is tender, and then adding a blend of spices, such as cumin, coriander, chili powder, and garlic, to give the dish its characteristic flavor. The finished dish is thick and flavorful, and it is often served with roti or naan bread, and garnished with fresh herbs and lemon wedges. Nihari is a popular dish in Pakistan, and it is often served at special occasions and celebrations.

Ingredients

Here is a general recipe for nihari that you can try:

  • 1 pound of beef or lamb
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon of ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon of chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon of turmeric
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups of water
  • 2 tablespoons of ghee or vegetable oil
  • Fresh cilantro and lemon wedges for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat the ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger paste, and saut√© for 3-4 minutes, until the onion is softened.
  2. Add the beef or lamb to the pot and stir well to mix it with the onion mixture.
  3. Add the cumin, coriander, chili powder, turmeric, and salt to the pot, and stir well to coat the meat with the spices.
  4. Pour the water into the pot and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and let the nihari simmer for 2-3 hours, or until the meat is tender and falls apart easily.
  5. Serve the nihari hot, garnished with fresh cilantro and lemon wedges. It can be eaten with roti or naan bread on the side.
